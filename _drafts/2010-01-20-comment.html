The people I spoke to over the phone and email were friendly and helpful.

Getting there is pretty easy, head to Rathdowney, then toward Boonah on the Boonah-Rathdowney Rd, then follow the Mt Barney Lodge signs. If you have a GPS, I was unable to find Upper Logan Road to enter my address, so use the GPS coordinates of -28.256506, 152.747396 and manually select the road from there, this is the start of the dirt road, the last few kilometers are dirt road, pretty compacted so no worries of getting bogged. You can even travel along the dirt road on Google Street View.




View Larger Map

The camp grounds are broken into 3 groups, 1 and 2 are drive in and setup, you can park next to your tent. Camp ground 3 you need to park up near the toilet block and carry your stuff into the camp area. There are fire pits scattered around each camp area, and while you can buy wood for $10 bundle at the camp ground, and slightly cheaper if you drive 10min further down the Boonah-Rathdowney Rd, it's mostly hardwood, great for heat and cooking, but if you just want a good fire to sit around, bring some wood from home.


View Mt Barney Lodge Camp Ground in a larger map


When you get there make sure you ask them about all the activities you can do, there are a wide range of them and they are reasonably priced.

North-west corner of Camp Ground 3.

Behind our camp area just down a short hill to river.

The river, just after rain so bit mucky, but was nice for swimming day after.

Follow the river upstream and you'll find a swimming hole complete with rope swing. check water under for debris first.
The Lower Portals walk in the Mt Barney National Park is pretty close and reasonably easy if your semi-fit. I'm a computer programmer, so in an office chair all day and I managed.

Mt Barney Lower Portals Walk, not much to see along the path.

Most of the Lower Portals way is path like this, reasonably well defined.

On the Lower Portals you need to cross this river, and walk about 250m to get to a water hole, rest of my team stayed at river crossing so I couldn't explore waterhole better to maybe see waterfall.
On our way to the Upper Portals Walk we stopped at the Mt May Reserve thinking this was the starting point of the walk, the road had a sign warning 4wd Only. We followed another group across a small bridge and followed the creek, after a few creek crossings 5min in we can to a water hole and waterfall, very nice, but not the walk we were after.
After looking at the map we found out that you need to go along the 4wd only track to get to the start of this walk. By the looks of things that's 4km of waking just to get to the walk without a 4wd.
