--- layout: post title: PICAXE 08M STARTER PACK date: '2008-11-18T19:20:00.003+10:00' author: sacah tags: - picaxe modified_time: '2008-12-21T08:49:08.400+10:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-4605009901196846959.post-8690233311903665614 blogger_orig_url: http://www.sacah.net/2008/11/picaxe-08m-starter-pack.html --- After my attempts at using the HC08, I thought I'd try my hand at PICAXEs to get back into the swing of micro-controllers. I purchased the PICAXE 08M STARTER PACK from http://www.wiltronics.com.au/catalogue/shop.php?cid=177763 put it all together and hey presto this one actually works, unlike the HC08.

If you try this starter kit, be sure to look at the assembly instructions at http://www.rev-ed.co.uk/picaxe/ click the Schools Experimenter picture to view a very helpful PDF for putting it together and your first program.

I am finding that because the PICAXE is used in schools and by hobbyist there is a wealth of examples out there to learn from.


What was my problem with the HC08?
I bought it from a company in Australia, who I think had it sitting on shelf for many years as the software was out of date and didn't work downloading the examples to the demo board. So I downloaded the latest software and the example code for my HC8 didn't work with the new software, but the new examples didn't work with my HC08. So I was stuck without being able to get anything but 1 basic LED flashing example to work. Not having done this for a few years I'm left unsure if it's me just having forgotten something or if the demo board is faulty. Hopefully one day I'll have a logic analyzer and I'll might be able to find what's happening.
